Navigate Complex Privacy Regulations

Privacy laws are multiplying. Each jurisdiction has different requirements. Non-compliance means fines, lawsuits, and reputation damage.

GDPR (Europe)

European Union's General Data Protection Regulation

  • Applies to ANY company processing EU resident data
  • Fines up to €20M or 4% of global revenue
  • Requires: consent, data minimization, right to erasure
  • Complex cross-border transfer rules

CCPA/CPRA (California)

California Consumer Privacy Act & Rights Act

  • Applies to companies doing business in California
  • Fines up to $7,500 per intentional violation
  • Private right of action for data breaches
  • Requires: opt-out, deletion rights, disclosure

Surveillance Laws (Federal/State)

Wiretap Act, biometric privacy, geolocation tracking

  • Federal wiretap laws (Title III)
  • Biometric privacy (BIPA in Illinois, others)
  • Geolocation tracking restrictions
  • Two-party consent for recording

Emerging Regulations

State privacy laws, AI regulations, international

  • Colorado, Virginia, Utah privacy acts
  • EU AI Act (2026 enforcement)
  • UK GDPR, Brazil LGPD, others
  • Sector-specific: HIPAA, FERPA, FCRA
